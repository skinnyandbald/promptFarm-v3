- **Negotiation and Prioritization**  
  Agents employ **goal prioritization**, utility-based scoring, and negotiation protocols (e.g., contract net algorithms, game-theoretic strategies) to resolve conflicting objectives[3].   The "spine" refers to the foundational values or operational constraints encoded in the agent or council system. These guide decision weighting and prohibit compromising on critical principles, supporting both authenticity and integrity in outcomes[1][3]. Hierarchical architectures help by setting systemic constraints that lower-level agents must respect, ensuring consistent adherence to council-wide rules or values[3].

- **Psychological Factors and Voice Distinctiveness**  
  Recent research models agent behavior through psychological frameworks—such as the Big Five personality traits—to examine how **openness, conscientiousness, extraversion, agreeableness, and neuroticism** affect conflict response[4]. Agents with high openness may accept diverse viewpoints, enhancing council adaptability and authentic deliberation, while more cautious agents maintain skepticism. - **Council Systems for Collective Decision-Making**  
  In council architectures, multiple agents with varying principles and perspectives are coordinated to reconcile differences, distribute authority, and reach consensus or principled compromise. Advanced systems employ simulation environments where agents debate, negotiate, and adapt strategies, often with dynamic weighting of input based on expertise, trustworthiness, or relevance[5]. Transformer-based dialogue models and federated learning are emerging to facilitate privacy and inclusiveness in multi-agent councils[5].

- **Maintaining Authenticity and Distinctiveness**  
  Authenticity is maintained by encoding explicit values or guiding rules for each agent, ensuring their actions and outputs reflect both systemic and individual distinctiveness. Multi-agent systems rigorously test for edge cases—like sudden priority shifts or data inconsistencies—to expose and correct failures in integrity or voice blending[3][5].
